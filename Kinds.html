PRODUCERS

These organisms synthesise their own food by photosynthesis.

Example: All green plants and blue green algae. However, in the strict sense, the plants (or the producers) donâ€™t produce energy. They only convert solar energy into chemical energy of food (Transducers).

CONSUMERS

These organisms are dependent upon other organism for food. Example: All animals including humans. Though in a general sense, we prepare our own food by cooking the raw materials used are obtained from either plants or animals which again gain energy from plants only.

Consumers can be classified as:

  • Herbivores (Primary consumers)
  • Carnivores (Secondary consumers)
  • Omnivores and parasite

DECOMPOSERS

These consume, and thus decompose, the dead remains of other organisms (both autotrophs and heterotrophs). For example, certain bacteria and fungi break down the complex organic compounds present in the dead organisms into simple substances. Through the decomposers the elements (minerals) enter the earth/soil again, which are in turn again taken up by the plants with the help of their roots. Thus, the cycle of exchange of matter continues(Biogeochemical cycles). By their activity, the decomposers also maintain soil fertility.
